FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about how the service works, what helpers can help with, and what is outside the scope of this early trial service.

About the service

What is this service?

This service connects foreigners in Hsinchu City and Zhubei with local bilingual helpers for in-person support with everyday tasks.

Examples include appointments, admin-related situations, practical errands, and phone calls during a session.

What areas do you cover?

At the moment, the service is available only in Hsinchu City and Zhubei.

Is this an online or in-person service?

This is an in-person service only. The helper and client meet at a specific destination to deal with the task together.

Phone calls may be made during the session if needed, but the service itself is based on meeting in person.

What kinds of tasks can helpers assist with?

Helpers can assist with many everyday situations, including:

  • appointments
  • admin visits
  • translation support during a meeting
  • practical errands
  • phone calls during the session
  • general help navigating common local processes

If you are unsure whether your task is suitable, you can still submit a request and we will review it manually.

What kinds of tasks are not allowed?

Helpers cannot assist with:

  • anything illegal
  • making decisions for the client
  • signing documents for the client
  • handling money on behalf of the client
  • legal advice
  • medical advice or medical decision-making
  • court appearances
  • police or law enforcement interactions

Bookings and pricing

How long is a session?

The base session length is 1 hour.

If both the client and the helper agree, the session may be extended in 30-minute increments.

How much does it cost?

The current trial pricing is:

  • NT$400 for the first hour
  • NT$200 for each additional 30 minutes, if both sides agree to continue

All requests are reviewed manually before confirmation.

How do I book a helper?

You can submit a request through the booking form on the website.

During this early trial, requests are reviewed manually before they are confirmed.

Can I make a same-day booking?

No. During this early trial, short-notice bookings are not available. Please give us enough time to review and arrange your request.

How does payment work?

Payment details will be confirmed as part of the booking process during this early trial.

What happens if I need to cancel?

If your plans change, please contact us as soon as possible. Cancellation handling for the early trial will be confirmed during the booking process.

Boundaries and safety

Can the helper make phone calls for me?

A helper may assist with phone calls during the in-person session if needed. However, the helper is there to support communication and practical understanding, not to act independently on your behalf outside the agreed session.

Can the helper sign documents for me or make decisions for me?

No.

Helpers cannot sign documents for clients, make decisions for clients, handle money on behalf of clients, or represent clients in legal, medical, or official decision-making situations.

Can the helper come with me to a bank, hospital, or government office?

Sometimes, yes — if the task fits within the service boundaries. Each request is reviewed manually.

Can I request a male or female helper?

You can state a preference in the booking form. We will try to take that into account where possible, but we cannot guarantee every preference in every case.

Are helpers professional translators?

Not necessarily. This service is designed for everyday practical support and bilingual help in real-world situations.

For helpers

How are helpers chosen?

During this early trial, helper applications are reviewed manually. We look for people who can communicate well, behave professionally, and handle everyday practical situations responsibly.

How do I apply to become a helper?

You can apply through the helper registration form on the website. Shortlisted applicants will be invited to a short video call.

How much do helpers get paid?

For the current trial, helpers are paid NT$250 per hour for completed bookings.

If a session is extended by agreement, additional time is paid proportionally.
